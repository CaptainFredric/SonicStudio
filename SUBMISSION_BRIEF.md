# SonicStudio Submission Brief

## Short pitch

SonicStudio is a browser-native composition studio that combines sequencing, arrangement, sample slicing, MIDI interoperability, print analysis, and project recovery in one local-first workflow.

## What makes it strong for the challenge

1. It is a real product surface with working studio behavior.
2. It has meaningful technical depth across audio, editing, project state, and export.
3. It demonstrates iterative engineering rather than a thin wrapper around one model call.
4. It is usable in the browser with real workflow exits like MIDI import and export, WAV bounce, checkpoints, and sound recall.

## Best reviewer route

1. Open the app and use the submission guide.
2. Wake audio and press Play.
3. Load `Night Transit` and open `Song` view.
4. Load `Beat Lab` and open `Grid` view.
5. Open `Output` and show MIDI export or WAV bounce targets.
6. Open `Workspace` and show recovery checkpoints.

Recommended share URL:

`https://captainfredric.github.io/SonicStudio/?demo=night-transit&view=song&guide=1`

## Competitive read

Relative to showcase projects with stronger static marketing pages, SonicStudio wins on product depth.

Relative to more visually cinematic projects like `God's Eye`, SonicStudio needs more reviewer guidance and stronger framing, which is why the submission guide now exists inside the app.

Relative to narrower tools like `ContentForge`, SonicStudio has a bigger surface and higher upside, but it also asks more from the reviewer. The submission path needs to keep that complexity legible.

## Honest positioning

SonicStudio is strongest as:

1. a browser-native music composition and arrangement tool
2. a technically ambitious local-first studio workflow
3. a project that shows serious product iteration beyond visual prototyping

It is weaker as:

1. a one-glance marketing screenshot
2. a simple thirty-second product pitch
3. a fully mature DAW replacement today

## Recommended submission framing

Use language close to this:

> SonicStudio is a browser-native composition studio I built to make real music creation possible in a local-first web workflow. It combines sequencing, arrangement editing, sample slicing, MIDI import and export, WAV bounce analysis, and recovery checkpoints in one product surface. I focused on turning a rough groovebox into something closer to a practical creative tool.
